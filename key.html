MIT Collaboration Report — Key & Documentation

Reference for every page, column, filter, and counting rule in this report. Use the sidebar to jump to any section.

1Overview

This report explores the collaborative relationships across units — both within and external to the Massachusetts Institute of Technology — built from the Academic Analytics comparative database AAD2024-2904. It covers roughly 1,270 MIT scholars and 16,700 distinct works across seven collaboration types. Note that scholars with no collaborations are not included.

These six pages sit together under “AAD2024 Snapshot” in the shared top nav — International & Industry (a separate dataset/pipeline; see its own Overview page) has its own group alongside it, with Peer Comparisons and International Funding as upcoming groups. Each AAD2024 Snapshot page offers a different lens on the same underlying data:

  • Details — a detailed view of the entire dataset, with columns that can be shown concatenated or split into separate rows, and a one-row-per-work option for viewing each work's full collaborator roster.
  • Counts — distinct-works totals per college/school, department, or person.
  • Network — force-directed graph of people, anchored units, departments, or colleges/schools.
  • Chord — college/school-level flows, plus an anchored partner view.
  • Matrix — unit × unit heat map of shared distinct works by collaboration type.
  • Insights — degree and betweenness centrality rankings for scholars and departments.

2Core concepts

2a. Unit Type & anchors

All three unit types are shown: Department, Program, and Medical. The Unit Type picker controls which units “anchor” the current view.

  • Department — department-anchored; Department columns shown, Program columns hidden.
  • Program(s) — program-anchored; Program(s) columns shown, Department columns hidden.
  • All — both, in separate columns.
  • Medical units are always included in the loaded data; the Medical column appears in Medical or All mode.

An anchor is the unit whose scholars a view is centered on. A person affiliated with several units counts as a member of whichever unit is anchored — a joint Physics/Math person is “a Physics person” when Physics is selected.

2b. Scope & relationship

Every faculty–collaborator pair has one relationship, always relative to the anchored unit:

within the collaborator shares the anchor unit itself
across the collaborator is in a different MIT unit
inter the collaborator is external to MIT

The Scope control filters by relationship: Within Unit, Across Units, Intra (within + across, i.e. all-MIT), Across Institutions, or All.

Multi-affiliation rule

A collaborator with several affiliations takes their most specific relationship — within beats across beats inter. If any of their units matches the anchor, they are within, full stop. This rule is applied identically on every page.

2c. Works vs. partner-summed counts

Two counting conventions appear in this report, and both are correct under their stated definitions:

  • Distinct works — each work counted once (Details, Counts, Matrix).
  • Partner-summed works — works summed over partner units, so a work shared with three partner units counts three times (Chord anchored view; the portal’s convention).
Never sum rows

The Details grain is one row per faculty × work × collaborator — summing rows massively overcounts works. Use the distinct-works figure in the status bar, or the counts pages.

2d. Caps: Top N & max collaborators

Max collaborators / cap authors per work hides very large consortium works (some physics collaborations run to hundreds of authors); available caps are 20 / 50 / 100 / no limit. Top N limits how many anchor scholars or partners a visualization draws, keeping the highest-degree ones; a badge reports how many were hidden.

3b. Filters

  • Unit Type — see 2a; also drives which unit columns appear.
  • Select College/School → Select Department(s) — cascading: choosing a college/school narrows the unit list.
  • Select Discipline(s) / Select Collab Discipline(s) — restrict by faculty-side or collaborator-side discipline.
  • Scope — see 2b.
  • Interdisciplinary — Yes keeps rows where faculty and collaborator disciplines differ.
  • Search / Year range / Detail contains / Max collaborators per work — free filters.
  • Faculty & Collab column panels — Show toggles add optional columns; Concat joins multiple values into one cell vs. one row per value.
  • Clear Filters resets everything; Download Data (CSV) exports all filtered rows (not just the visible page) with the on-screen columns.

3c. One row per work

Collapses the table to one row per distinct work. Filters choose which works qualify; each qualifying work then lists its complete roster — every person exactly once, MIT collaborators and external collaborators in separate columns, MIT faculty first. Long lists show the first 10 people with an expandable “+ N more”. Each person shows their unit(s) beneath their name (per the Unit Type mode) and full affiliations in a hover tooltip. Relationship pills appear only when a unit is selected in the Department filter — they are meaningless without a reference unit.

4Counts

Honest distinct-works totals — no pairs model, so every column always adds up work-for-work. Choose a level to change what one row represents:

  • Institution — a single fixed summary card for all of MIT; the top-line total everything else on the page rolls up to.
  • College / School — one row per college/school; a work counts here if any scholar in that college/school is on it.
  • Department (or Program/Medical unit, depending on Unit Type mode) — one row per unit; a work counts here if any scholar in that unit is on it.
  • Person — one row per scholar; a work counts here if that scholar is a co-author on it.

Every row, at every level, shows the same five columns:

  • Within — the work stayed entirely inside the entity (e.g. all authors are in the same department).
  • Across — the work reaches at least one other MIT entity of the same kind.
  • Inter — the work involves at least one external institution.
  • Intra — Within + Across combined, i.e. every work that stays inside MIT.
  • All — every distinct work touching the entity, regardless of relationship.

Use this page when you need totals that add up correctly — see 2c for how this differs from the partner-summed convention used elsewhere in this report.

5Network

Force-directed collaboration graph with four levels, ordered big to small: College / School, Department, Anchor (one unit and its partner units), and Person (anchored scholars and their individual collaborators).

  • Node color — faculty rank at Person level (legend on-page); school colors at the global levels. Node size scales with works/degree. A thick border marks the selected unit’s scholars.
  • Edge color — relationship: green within, gold across MIT, purple external.
  • Interaction — hover for a tooltip; click a node for a detail panel with works by collaboration type; scroll to zoom; drag to pan; Fit view resets.
  • Controls — Unit Type, Anchor unit, Scope (default All), Top N nodes, cap authors/work.

6Chord

Two modes. College / School Overview draws MIT’s seven colleges/schools as arcs, with ribbon thickness proportional to the distinct works shared between each pair of schools (same pair-based definition as the Matrix). Anchored View centers one unit and fans out its partners, using the partner-summed convention (see 2c). Hover a ribbon or arc to isolate it; Top N and author-cap controls apply in anchored mode.

7Matrix

Unit × unit heat map. Each cell counts distinct works with at least one distinct-person pair connecting the two units (MIT-side collaborators only); the diagonal is a unit’s within-unit works. Cells can be filtered to a single collaboration type, capped by authors per work, viewed at Department/Program or College / School level, and sorted by total or alphabetically.

Cells are not additive

A work connecting three units appears in all three pairwise cells — summing cells overcounts. Use Counts for additive totals.

8Insights

Connector rankings computed on the person-level collaboration graph: Degree Centrality (distinct collaborators; a degree-cap toggle recomputes under author caps), Betweenness Centrality (how often a scholar sits on shortest paths between others — bridge-builders), and a Departments roll-up. Filter by department, choose how many rows to show, and click a scholar for their profile and top co-authors.
